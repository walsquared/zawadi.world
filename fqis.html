Installing Linux Part 1: Getting ready

July 19, 2026

Step 1: Backup your data

Installing a new operating system will wipe your hard drive, so you want to make sure all your important data is in a safe place, like a USB, an external hard drive, or on the cloud.

Step 2: Check for Linux versions of your apps

Some programs are not available on Linux. Make a list of your most used apps, programs, or games, and see if they have Linux versions.

Step 3: Choose a distro

Each “distro” or flavour of LInux fills a specific niche. If you’re not sure which to decide, I’ll be showing you how to install Linux Mint, since it’s beginner-friendly.

Lastly, you’ll need a USB stick. It needs to have at least 8GB of storage, and will be needed for part 2!
